# Life / Death Cover — Provider Comparison Guide

> **When to use this file:** Load when client needs death benefit, terminal illness cover, or estate planning. Cross-reference `_recommendation-guide.md` for channel and tax guidance.

---

## Product Overview

Life cover (also called death cover or term life insurance) pays a lump sum on the insured's death or terminal illness diagnosis. It is the most widely held retail insurance product in Australia.

**Key decision factors:**
- Sum insured adequacy (income replacement + debt clearance + dependant provision)
- Terminal illness definition (typically 12 or 24 months to live)
- Premium structure (stepped vs level)
- Claims acceptance rate
- Policy flexibility (partial benefits, future insurability)

## Provider Deep-Dive

### TAL Life (Dai-ichi Group)

**Profile:** Australia's largest life insurer by market share. Underwriter for many retail advisers and industry super fund group insurance.

**Strengths:**
- Largest claims-paying experience in Australia
- Strong reinsurance backing (Hannover Re)
- Broad distribution via independent advisers
- Accelerated death benefit (terminal illness) with 12-month diagnosis
- Financial planning benefit: up to 1% of sum insured (max $5,000) to cover the cost of financial advice after a claim

**Weaknesses:**
- Premium competitiveness varies by age/health band
- No built-in premium freeze option

**Best for:** High-sum-insured needs, clients with complex health histories who need underwriting flexibility, adviser-distributed cases.

**Unique features:**
- Accidental death benefit rider available
- Guaranteed future insurability on key life events (marriage, mortgage, new child)
- Premium holiday (waiver) provisions

---

### AIA Australia

**Profile:** Second-largest retail life insurer. Known for AIA Vitality wellness program.

**Strengths:**
- AIA Vitality: premium discounts up to 30% for healthy lifestyle (gym, health checks, steps)
- Highest advance payment benefit ($25,000 funeral advance)
- Strong direct-to-consumer and group presence
- Robust digital claims lodgment

**Weaknesses:**
- Vitality premium discounts require active engagement — clients who lapse healthy behaviour may lose discounts
- Premium rates without Vitality discount can be less competitive

**Best for:** Health-conscious clients willing to engage with wellness programs, younger demographics who want premium reward structures.

**Unique features:**
- AIA Vitality: up to 30% ongoing premium discount
- Premium waiver during total disability built-in (some products)
- Crisis recovery benefit on some products

---

### Zurich / OnePath Life

**Profile:** Swiss-backed insurer with strong Australian retail presence. Known for policy certainty features.

**Strengths:**
- **24-month terminal illness definition** (most generous — gives clients more time and certainty)
- **Premium freeze option** (lock premium at current level and reduce sum insured instead of stepping up)
- Automatic CPI indexation on some products (inflation protection without opt-in)
- Strong rehabilitation and recovery benefits

**Weaknesses:**
- Distribution primarily through advisers — less direct access
- Market share smaller than TAL/AIA

**Best for:** Clients who want maximum terminal illness certainty (24 months), those concerned about future premium increases (premium freeze), older clients approaching retirement who want level-style control.

**Unique features:**
- Premium freeze: sum insured reduces automatically to hold premium flat
- 24-month terminal illness (not 12 months like most competitors)
- Specific illness benefits on some bundled policies

---

### MLC Life (Nippon Life)

**Profile:** Backed by Japan's Nippon Life. Strong group/employer-sponsored distribution.

**Strengths:**
- Large group insurance presence (employer-sponsored)
- Competitive premiums for standard risk occupations in group schemes
- Integration with MLC super fund platform

**Weaknesses:**
- Retail policy expiry at age 70 on group products (vs 99 for retail peers)
- Less competitive in individual adviser-distributed market
- Reduced future insurability options on group products

**Best for:** Clients whose employer uses MLC as the default group insurer, corporate/group schemes.

---

### Resolution Life (AMP Legacy)

**Profile:** Legacy retail life insurer managing AMP's closed book. Not actively writing significant new business.

**Weaknesses:**
- Legacy systems — policy administration older
- Reduced innovation in product features
- Claims management service quality concerns noted in ASIC reviews

**Best for:** Existing AMP policyholders reviewing whether to retain grandfathered terms. **Caution: replacing an existing Resolution Life policy may sacrifice grandfathered terms — always check.**

## Life Cover Recommendation Framework

### Step 1 — Calculate the Life Need

Use the DIME method or human life value:

```
Life Need = Income Replacement + Debt Clearance + Future Dependant Costs − Existing Assets
```

**Income replacement:** Annual net income × years to retirement (or youngest dependant age − current age)
**Debt clearance:** Mortgage balance + other debts
**Dependant provision:** Cost of raising dependants to independence (often $50,000–$100,000 per child as a simplified estimate)
**Less:** Existing liquid assets, existing life cover, expected super death benefit

### Step 2 — Select Channel

| Situation | Recommended Channel |
|---|---|
| High sum insured (>$1M), complex needs | Retail via adviser |
| Standard risk, budget-conscious | Group / super fund first |
| Young, healthy, simple needs | Direct or retail |
| Pre-existing health conditions | Retail (underwriting flexibility) |

### Step 3 — Select Provider

| Priority | Best Provider |
|---|---|
| Largest claims-paying history | TAL |
| Premium reduction program (wellness) | AIA (Vitality) |
| Longest terminal illness window (24m) | Zurich |
| Premium certainty / freeze | Zurich |
| Group/employer-integrated | MLC |

## Claims Scenarios and Definitions

### Terminal Illness Definition

**Standard (TAL, AIA, MLC):** A medical practitioner certifies that despite reasonable medical treatment, the client is likely to die within **12 months**.

**Zurich:** Extended to **24 months** — client has more time to plan, access funds, and manage affairs without the rushed 12-month window.

> Adviser note: Always confirm which definition applies before recommending Zurich vs alternatives for terminal illness priority clients.

### Suicide and Self-Harm Exclusion

All providers apply a **13-month exclusion** for suicide or intentional self-harm under the Insurance Contracts Act 1984 (Cth). After 13 months, claims for suicide are payable.

### Non-Disclosure

If the client failed to disclose material facts at application, the insurer may:
- Void the policy from inception (no claim paid)
- Reduce the benefit proportionally
- Vary the policy terms

> Key risk: Non-disclosure discovered at claim time is the most common grounds for denial. Thorough fact-find is essential.

## Tax Treatment — Life Cover

| Situation | Tax Treatment |
|---|---|
| Death benefit paid to **tax dependant** (spouse, minor child) | **Tax-free** |
| Death benefit paid to **non-tax-dependant** (adult child) from **super** | Taxed at up to **15%** (taxable component) |
| Death benefit paid to **non-tax-dependant** from **retail/outside super** | Generally **tax-free** (as it is capital, not income) |
| Terminal illness benefit | **Tax-free** regardless of channel |
| Premiums paid personally (outside super) | **NOT tax-deductible** |
| Premiums paid through super | Funded from concessional contributions (15% contributions tax applies to contributions, but premiums themselves are not an additional deduction) |

> Key compliance point: If client has non-dependant beneficiaries (e.g., adult children), retail/outside-super ownership is preferred to avoid super's 15% tax on the taxable component of death benefits.

---

## Common Life Cover Exclusions

- Suicide/self-harm within first 13 months
- Death resulting from war or armed conflict
- Aviation (as pilot or crew) — unless loading applied
- Illegal drug use or criminal activity
- Material non-disclosure at application
- Pre-existing undisclosed conditions
