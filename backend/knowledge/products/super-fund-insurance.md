# Life Insurance Inside Super — Industry Fund Product Guide

> **When to use this file:** Load when the client holds or is considering life/TPD/IP insurance inside a superannuation fund. Use alongside `life-cover-comparison.md` for split-strategy comparisons.

---

## How Group Insurance Inside Super Works

Industry (and some retail) super funds offer **default group insurance** — life (death), TPD, and income protection — automatically to eligible members without individual underwriting up to the automatic acceptance limit (AAL).

**Key advantages:**
- No medical underwriting required up to the AAL (typically $500k–$2M life cover depending on fund)
- Premiums deducted from super balance — no impact on take-home pay
- 15% super tax environment reduces effective cost vs. post-tax retail premiums
- Default cover starts on fund entry — no waiting for policy approval

**Key limitations:**
- Cover ends if super account becomes inactive (PYS Act 2019)
- TPD definition is typically "any occupation" inside super (SIS Act constraint)
- Cover ceiling lower than retail for very high sum-insured needs
- Benefit paid via trustee — subject to SIS dependency rules and potential tax on taxable component for non-dependants

## Increasing Cover Inside Your Existing Industry Fund

Most industry funds allow members to apply for **voluntary increases** above the default level:

1. **Without medical underwriting** — up to the fund's Automatic Acceptance Limit (AAL), typically applied at fund entry or during a limited window after joining. Example: AustralianSuper AAL is often $1.5M–$2M life cover for new members under 60.

2. **With medical underwriting (loadings/exclusions may apply)** — for increases above the AAL, the fund's group insurer (usually TAL, AIA, or MetLife as underwriter) assesses the application. John's clean health profile (BMI ~25.9, no conditions) would likely be accepted at standard rates.

**Recommendation for John (age 42, industry fund, $250k current cover, $1.78M shortfall):**
- Step 1: Apply to increase life cover inside current fund to ~$1.5M–$2M (within or just above AAL).
- Step 2: If the fund's cover ceiling is insufficient or premiums become uncompetitive, implement a **split strategy**: retain $1M–$1.5M inside super + purchase $500k–$800k retail life cover outside super for flexibility.

## Fund Switching for Better Insurance

If John's current industry fund has a low AAL or limited cover options, switching funds may be appropriate:

| Priority | Recommended Fund |
|---|---|
| High AAL (large increases without medical) | AustralianSuper, Aware Super, Australian Retirement Trust |
| IT / Professional white-collar workers | CareSuper, Aware Super, Hostplus |
| Competitive premiums for age 40–45 | AustralianSuper, Hostplus, Australian Retirement Trust |
| Strong claims handling | AustralianSuper (largest volume), Aware Super |

> **Caution:** Switching funds to access better insurance may:
> - Restart the PYS inactivity clock
> - Require re-underwriting above the new fund's AAL
> - Affect existing benefit conditions — confirm portability clauses before switching

## Tax Comparison: Inside vs Outside Super (for John)

| | Inside Super | Outside Super (retail) |
|---|---|---|
| Premium funding | Pre-tax super contributions (15% tax) | Post-tax personal income (37% marginal rate) |
| Effective premium cost | Lower (contributions tax already applied) | Higher out-of-pocket |
| Death benefit tax (spouse/dependant) | Tax-free | Tax-free |
| Death benefit tax (non-dependant) | Up to 15% on taxable component | Tax-free (capital receipt) |
| Policy flexibility | Controlled by trustee | Full client control |

**For John (37% marginal rate, dependant spouse + children):**
Inside super is materially more cost-efficient. Premiums are funded from pre-tax contributions, and beneficiaries (spouse and minor children) receive death benefits tax-free.
