# TPD Cover — Provider Comparison Guide

> **When to use this file:** Load when client cannot work and disability is expected to be permanent. Cross-reference `_recommendation-guide.md` for channel, tax, and regulatory rules. For deep TPD assessment (SIS compliance, definition quality, lapse, AFCA paths), use the `tpd_policy_assessment` tool.

---

## Product Overview

Total & Permanent Disability (TPD) cover pays a lump sum if the insured is permanently unable to work or perform activities of daily living (depending on definition). It is the most litigated insurance product in Australia (ASIC Rep 633).

**Key decision factors:**
- **Definition quality** — own-occupation vs any-occupation vs ADL (activities of daily living)
- **Claims acceptance rate** (own-occ: ~88%; any-occ: ~80%; ADL: ~40% — ASIC data)
- Super vs retail placement (tax and SIS compliance implications)
- Premium structure and affordability
- Waiting period (typically 3–6 months of continuous disability)

---

## TPD Definition Tiers (Ranked Best to Worst)

| Rank | Definition Type | Description | Typical Claims Rate | Available Channel |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Own Occupation** | Cannot perform the duties of YOUR specific occupation | ~88% | Retail only (outside super) |
| 2 | **Modified Own Occupation** | Own-occ with some carve-outs for hazardous duties | ~85% | Retail (some products) |
| 3 | **Any Occupation** | Cannot perform ANY occupation suited to education, training, experience | ~80% | Retail + Super |
| 4 | **Activities of Daily Living (ADL)** | Cannot perform 2 of 5 ADLs (bathing, dressing, eating, mobility, continence) | ~40% | Super / Direct only |
| 5 | **Home Duties** | Cannot perform domestic duties — for non-working spouses | ~60% | Retail (specific products) |

> **Critical rule:** Own-occupation TPD cannot be held inside superannuation (SIS Reg 6.01 — super must only provide benefits on conditions of release consistent with permanent incapacity per s68AA). Use retail/outside-super for own-occupation.

## Provider Deep-Dive

### TAL Life — TPD

**Strengths:**
- Market-leading experience with complex TPD claims
- Own-occupation definition available to most white-collar occupations (class AA–B)
- Partial TPD benefit: percentage of sum insured for partial permanent disability
- Rehabilitation benefit: up to $50,000 for vocational retraining
- **Financial planning benefit:** helps fund adviser cost post-claim

**Weaknesses:**
- Own-occupation not available to manual/hazardous occupation classes (C, D)
- Stepped premium increases significantly after age 40

**Occupation Class Guide (TAL):**

| Class | Description | Own-Occ Available |
|---|---|---|
| AA | Professional / white-collar (doctor, lawyer, accountant) | Yes |
| A | Skilled office / supervisory | Yes |
| B | Light manual / trades (electrician, carpenter) | Yes |
| C | Heavy manual (bricklayer, miner) | No (any-occ only) |
| D | Hazardous (roof tiler, scaffolder) | No (any-occ only) |

---

### AIA Australia — TPD

**Strengths:**
- AIA Vitality discounts apply to TPD premiums (same wellness program as life cover)
- Strong own-occupation definition for class A–B
- TPD multi-benefit: partial payment for losing one limb or sight, plus full payment for total disability
- Rehabilitation benefit and personal support benefit

**Weaknesses:**
- Vitality dependency for best pricing
- Own-occupation carve-outs in modified definition for some occupation types

---

### Zurich / OnePath — TPD

**Strengths:**
- Strong own-occupation definition language (clear, court-tested)
- **Inflation protection:** automatic CPI indexation on some products without annual opt-in
- Extended waiting period credit: existing waiting period from a previous policy may be credited on replacement (reduces gap risk)
- Rehabilitation services and return-to-work programs

**Weaknesses:**
- Distribution adviser-only (no direct)
- Premium competitiveness varies

**Best for:** Clients replacing an existing policy — waiting period credit reduces the risk of a coverage gap during the new waiting period.

---

### MLC Life — TPD

**Strengths:**
- Competitive for group/employer-sponsored TPD schemes
- Integration with MLC super platform
- Any-occupation definition standard for super-linked products

**Weaknesses:**
- Own-occupation definition limited in group schemes
- Retail product range less competitive vs TAL/AIA/Zurich for individual advice

## Regulatory Constraints — TPD in Super

### SIS Act Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Own-occupation in super** | Prohibited — super fund TPD must use any-occupation or ADL definition (SIS s68AA, Reg 6.01) |
| **MySuper default** | Any-occupation only for default (MySuper) products |
| **PMIF (2019)** | No automatic cover for members under 25 or balance < $6,000 |
| **Inactivity switch-off** | Cover cancelled after 16 months with no contributions/rollovers |
| **Auto-acceptance limit (Reg 4.07C)** | Automatic acceptance up to $100,000 sum insured for eligible new members |

### Tax on Super TPD Benefit (ITAA 1997 Div 295)

| Age at Claim | Tax Rate on Taxable Component |
|---|---|
| Under 60 | 22% (15% tax + 2% Medicare on taxable component) |
| 60 and over | **0%** (tax-free) |
| Disability super benefit (s995-1) | Tax-free element may apply if permanently incapacitated |

> If client is under 60 and claim size is large (e.g. $500,000), holding TPD inside super may result in ~$110,000 tax vs nil tax outside super. Always model this.

## TPD Claims Process and Red Flags

### Common Reasons for TPD Claim Denial (ASIC Rep 633 data)

1. **ADL definition:** ~60% denial rate — client must meet strict functional test, not just inability to work
2. **Non-disclosure at application** — undisclosed pre-existing conditions
3. **Partial recovery** — client recovers enough to perform "any" occupation
4. **Definition ambiguity** — "any occupation suited to education, training, experience" interpreted broadly by insurer
5. **Waiting period not satisfied** — claim lodged before 3 or 6 months continuous disability confirmed

### AFCA Escalation Path

If insurer denies TPD claim at Internal Dispute Resolution (IDR) stage:
- Client can escalate to AFCA (free to complainant)
- AFCA can award up to $3,000,000 in compensation
- Average AFCA TPD reversal rate: significant — AFCA frequently overturns denials where definition was ambiguously applied

## TPD vs Income Protection — When to Use Which

| Scenario | Recommended Cover |
|---|---|
| Disability is **permanent** — client will never work again | TPD (lump sum to clear debt, invest for income) |
| Disability is **temporary** — client may recover | Income Protection (monthly benefit) |
| Client has **both** risks | Both covers recommended (IP for during recovery, TPD for if permanent) |
| **Self-employed**, short income buffer | Income Protection priority (TPD as supplement) |
| **High mortgage** | TPD priority (lump sum clears debt immediately) |

---

## Tax Treatment — TPD Cover

| Situation | Tax Treatment |
|---|---|
| TPD paid **outside super** (retail) | **Tax-free** (capital lump sum) |
| TPD paid **inside super** (age < 60) | Taxed at ~22% on taxable component |
| TPD paid **inside super** (age ≥ 60) | **Tax-free** |
| Premiums — personal outside super | **NOT deductible** |
| Premiums — through super (employer/concessional) | Funded from concessional contributions (15% contributions tax on contributions) |
